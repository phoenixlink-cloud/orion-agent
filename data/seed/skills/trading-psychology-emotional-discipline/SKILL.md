SKILL: TRADING PSYCHOLOGY & EMOTIONAL DISCIPLINE

SKILL ID:       FX-SK-004
ROLE:           Professional Forex Trader
CATEGORY:       Psychology & Mindset
DIFFICULTY:     Advanced (arguably the hardest skill to master)
ESTIMATED TIME: Ongoing (lifetime practice)

DESCRIPTION
Managing the psychological and emotional challenges of trading, including fear,
greed, revenge trading, overconfidence, and the discipline to follow a trading
plan consistently through winning and losing streaks. Psychology is estimated
to account for 70-80% of trading success — a mediocre strategy with excellent
psychology will outperform a brilliant strategy with poor psychology every time.

THE PSYCHOLOGICAL CHALLENGES OF TRADING
FEAR: Prevents entering valid setups, causes premature exits, freezes decision-making
GREED: Leads to oversizing positions, moving targets, refusing to take profits
REVENGE TRADING: After a loss, immediately re-entering to "win it back" (impulsive, emotional)
OVERCONFIDENCE: After a winning streak, increasing risk, abandoning the plan
FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out): Chasing trades that have already moved, entering without setup
HOPE: Holding a losing trade hoping it will "come back" (moving or removing stops)
BOREDOM: Trading when there's no setup just to "be in the market"
CONFIRMATION BIAS: Seeing only evidence that supports your position, ignoring warning signs

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

STEP 1: DEVELOP A RULES-BASED TRADING PLAN
  The plan is your emotional shield. When emotions are high, the plan decides.
  Your plan must define:
  - WHEN to trade: Sessions, days (avoid Fridays? Mondays?)
  - WHAT to trade: Currency pairs, setups, timeframes
  - HOW to enter: Specific criteria that must ALL be met before entry
  - WHERE to place SL/TP: Based on technical levels, not arbitrary pips
  - HOW MUCH to risk: Position sizing formula (1-2% rule)
  - WHEN to exit: Take profit rules, trailing stop rules
  - WHEN NOT to trade: News events, emotional state, max loss hit

  The plan removes decision-making from the emotional moment.
  You make decisions BEFORE the market is open, when you're calm and rational.

STEP 2: PRE-TRADE CHECKLIST (Before every trade)
  Ask yourself:
  [ ] Does this setup match my trading plan criteria? (If not, don't trade)
  [ ] Have I done my multi-timeframe analysis? (Or am I reacting to a 5-min candle?)
  [ ] Is my entry, stop-loss, and take-profit clearly defined?
  [ ] Have I calculated the correct position size?
  [ ] Am I within my daily/weekly risk limits?
  [ ] Am I emotionally neutral? (Not angry, euphoric, scared, or bored)
  [ ] Would I take this trade if I had just lost 3 in a row? (If no, your sizing is too big)
  [ ] Am I chasing or is this a planned entry at a planned level?

  If ANY answer is "no" — DO NOT TAKE THE TRADE.

STEP 3: MANAGE EMOTIONS DURING TRADES
  While in a trade:
  - Set alerts at key levels and WALK AWAY from the screen
  - Watching every tick amplifies emotions (fear on every pullback)
  - Trust your analysis: You had a plan, the entry criteria were met, let it play out
  - Do not move your stop-loss further from entry (this is HOPE, not strategy)
  - Moving stop to breakeven after 1:1 RR is acceptable (reduces anxiety)
  - If you feel compelled to close early, ask: "Is this my plan or my emotions?"

STEP 4: HANDLE LOSING TRADES
  After a loss:
  - Accept it immediately. Losses are a COST OF DOING BUSINESS — like rent for a shop
  - Review: Did you follow the plan? If YES → it's a normal loss, move on
  - If NO (you broke a rule) → THAT is the problem, not the loss
  - Do NOT immediately re-enter to "make it back" (revenge trading)
  - Take a 15-30 minute break before looking at the next setup
  - If you've hit your daily loss limit, CLOSE THE PLATFORM and do something else

  Reframe losses:
  - "I lost " → "I risked  on a valid setup with positive expectancy"
  - Over 100 trades, if my expectancy is positive, individual losses don't matter
  - The best traders in the world lose 40-50% of their trades

STEP 5: HANDLE WINNING STREAKS
  After multiple wins:
  - DO NOT increase position sizes (overconfidence is as dangerous as fear)
  - Stick to the plan: Same risk %, same criteria, same discipline
  - Guard against: "I've figured out the market" (you haven't — nobody has)
  - Winning streaks are normal — they don't mean you're a genius
  - Losing streaks will follow — and you need to be emotionally prepared

STEP 6: BUILD MENTAL RESILIENCE
  Daily practices:
  - JOURNAL: Write 3-5 sentences about your emotional state and decision quality
  - MEDITATION: 10-15 minutes daily (trains focus, reduces reactive thinking)
  - EXERCISE: Physical activity reduces stress hormones, improves decision-making
  - SLEEP: 7-8 hours minimum — sleep deprivation destroys decision quality
  - ROUTINE: Same pre-market routine every day (creates calm, professional mindset)
  - AFFIRMATIONS: "I am a disciplined trader who follows the plan"
    (sounds cheesy, but repetition rewires neural pathways)

  Weekly review:
  - Rate your emotional discipline this week (1-10)
  - How many trades broke the plan? Why?
  - What triggered emotional decisions? (Specific events, times, situations)
  - What will you do differently next week?

STEP 7: RECOGNISE WHEN TO STOP
  Stop trading immediately if:
  - You've hit your daily loss limit
  - You feel angry, frustrated, or "need" to make money
  - You're entering trades without checking the plan
  - You're increasing position sizes after losses
  - You're moving stop-losses further from entry
  - You can't sleep because of open positions (you're risking too much)
  - You're checking your P&L every few minutes
  - Trading is negatively affecting your relationships or health

  Walk away. The market will be there tomorrow. Your capital might not be
  if you trade emotionally today.

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Trading journal: Edgewonk, TraderSync, Notion, spreadsheet
- Meditation apps: Headspace, Calm, Waking Up
- Books: "Trading in the Zone" (Mark Douglas), "The Disciplined Trader" (Mark Douglas),
  "Thinking, Fast and Slow" (Daniel Kahneman), "Atomic Habits" (James Clear)
- Trading plan template with pre-trade checklist
- Accountability: Trading mentor, peer group, or trading community
- Emotional state log (daily 1-10 rating with notes)

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Plan compliance: >= 95% of trades match the written trading plan
- Pre-trade checklist: Completed for every single trade
- Journal: Updated daily (every trade documented with emotional notes)
- Revenge trades: Zero (defined as entering within 15 min of a loss without plan criteria)
- Daily loss limit: Never exceeded
- Stop-loss compliance: 100% of trades have a stop-loss (never removed or widened)
- Emotional rating: Average >= 7/10 over the month (self-assessed)
- Weekly review: Completed every weekend without exception
