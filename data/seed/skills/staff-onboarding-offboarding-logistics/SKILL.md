SKILL: STAFF ONBOARDING & OFFBOARDING LOGISTICS

SKILL ID:       OA-SK-004
ROLE:           Office Administrator
CATEGORY:       HR Support & Logistics
DIFFICULTY:     Intermediate
ESTIMATED TIME: 4-8 hours per new starter/leaver

DESCRIPTION
Coordinating the physical and logistical aspects of bringing new employees
into the office and managing the departure process when staff leave. This
covers workspace setup, IT equipment provisioning, access card issuance,
welcome materials, orientation scheduling, and the reverse process for
departures including equipment collection and access revocation.

OBJECTIVES
1. 100% workspace readiness by Day 1 for every new starter
2. Complete the onboarding checklist within the first week
3. Process offboarding within 2 business days of departure date
4. Maintain a current asset register with zero discrepancies
5. Achieve >= 4.5/5.0 new starter satisfaction on onboarding experience
6. Zero security gaps from delayed access revocation on departures

ONBOARDING PROCEDURE

STEP 1: PRE-ARRIVAL PREPARATION (1-2 weeks before start date)
  Receive onboarding request from HR with:
  - New starter name, role, department, start date, manager name
  - IT requirements (laptop type, software, phone, email setup)
  - Seating preference or assigned desk

  Actions:
  [ ] Assign a desk/workspace — confirm with the manager
  [ ] Submit IT request: Laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset
  [ ] Request email account, system access, and software licences
  [ ] Request building access card and parking (if applicable)
  [ ] Order business cards (if applicable)
  [ ] Order name badge
  [ ] Prepare the welcome pack:
      - Company handbook, org chart, floor plan
      - Office guide (kitchen, bathrooms, parking, Wi-Fi password)
      - Stationery starter kit (notebook, pen, sticky notes)
      - Branded items (mug, t-shirt, lanyard — if available)
  [ ] Schedule Day 1 orientation agenda with HR, IT, and manager
  [ ] Notify reception of the new starter's arrival details
  [ ] Add to the internal directory and distribution lists
  [ ] Set up desk: Clean, equipment placed, welcome pack on desk

STEP 2: DAY 1 WELCOME
  Morning:
  - Greet the new starter at reception personally
  - Issue access card and explain security procedures
  - Escort to their desk; introduce to immediate team
  - Walk through the office: Kitchen, bathrooms, fire exits, meeting rooms
  - Hand over IT equipment with login credentials (sealed envelope)
  - Provide Wi-Fi access and printer setup instructions
  Afternoon:
  - Introduce to key contacts: Reception, IT helpdesk, facilities
  - Review the office guide and answer questions
  - Confirm upcoming orientation sessions (HR, IT, safety, team)
  - Check: Is the desk comfortable? Everything working? Any needs?

STEP 3: FIRST WEEK FOLLOW-UP
  - Day 2: Check all systems are working (email, phone, access)
  - Day 3: Ensure they have access to all required shared drives/tools
  - Day 5: Brief check-in: "Is everything set up? Anything you need?"
  - Update the asset register with all issued equipment
  - Confirm the onboarding checklist is 100% complete

STEP 4: 30-DAY CHECK-IN
  - Send a brief survey: How was the onboarding experience? (1-5 scale)
  - Address any outstanding issues or missing access
  - File the completed onboarding checklist

OFFBOARDING PROCEDURE

STEP 1: RECEIVE DEPARTURE NOTIFICATION (from HR)
  - Departure date, last working day, reason (resignation, termination, etc.)
  - Handover requirements and timeline

STEP 2: SCHEDULE OFFBOARDING ACTIONS
  Create a checklist with deadlines:
  [ ] IT: Schedule account deactivation for end of last working day
  [ ] Access: Schedule access card deactivation for end of last day
  [ ] Equipment: Schedule collection of laptop, phone, headset, keys
  [ ] Parking: Cancel parking allocation from departure date
  [ ] Distribution lists: Remove from all mailing lists and groups
  [ ] Directory: Remove from internal directory
  [ ] Business cards: Cancel any pending orders
  [ ] Forwarding: Set up email auto-reply and forwarding (per IT policy)

STEP 3: LAST DAY EXECUTION
  - Collect all company property:
    * Laptop and charger
    * Access card and keys
    * Phone and headset
    * Any other company-owned items
  - Have the departing employee sign the equipment return form
  - Update the asset register immediately
  - Verify IT has deactivated all accounts and access
  - Clear and clean the workspace for the next occupant

STEP 4: POST-DEPARTURE
  - Confirm all access has been revoked (building, systems, email)
  - Archive the employee's onboarding/offboarding file
  - Reallocate equipment to the pool or next new starter
  - Update seating chart and internal directory

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Onboarding checklist template (printable and digital)
- Offboarding checklist template
- Asset register (spreadsheet or asset management system)
- IT request form template
- Welcome pack materials and branded items
- Equipment return form
- Survey tool for onboarding feedback (Google Forms, SurveyMonkey)

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Workspace readiness: 100% by Day 1
- IT equipment functional: 100% by Day 1
- Onboarding checklist completion: 100% within first week
- Offboarding completion: 100% by end of last working day
- Access revocation: Same day as departure (zero delays)
- Asset register accuracy: 100%
- New starter satisfaction: >= 4.5/5.0
