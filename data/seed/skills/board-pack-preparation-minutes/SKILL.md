SKILL: BOARD PACK PREPARATION & MEETING MINUTES

SKILL ID:       EA-SK-003
ROLE:           Executive Assistant
CATEGORY:       Governance & Documentation
DIFFICULTY:     Advanced
ESTIMATED TIME: 8-16 hours per board meeting cycle

DESCRIPTION
Preparing comprehensive board meeting packs, coordinating board logistics,
recording accurate minutes, and tracking action items to completion. This
skill is critical for corporate governance and requires precision, discretion,
and the ability to synthesise complex information into clear documents.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

STEP 1: PRE-MEETING PREPARATION (2-3 weeks before)
  - Confirm date, time, venue (or video link) with all board members
  - Send save-the-date and confirm attendance/apologies
  - Collect agenda items from the Chairperson and executive team
  - Request reports/papers from: CEO, CFO, COO, Company Secretary
  - Set submission deadline: 10 business days before the meeting

STEP 2: COMPILE THE BOARD PACK (5 business days before)
  Standard board pack structure:
  1. Cover page: Meeting title, date, time, location, classification
  2. Agenda: Numbered items with time allocations and presenters
  3. Minutes of previous meeting (for approval)
  4. Action items register (status update on all open items)
  5. CEO Report: Strategic update, key achievements, risks
  6. CFO Report: Financial statements, budget vs actual, cash flow
  7. Committee Reports: Audit, Remuneration, Risk, Nominations
  8. Papers for Decision: Resolutions, approvals, policy changes
  9. Papers for Information: Market updates, regulatory changes
  10. AOB and next meeting date

  Formatting standards:
  - Consistent font, margins, and page numbering throughout
  - Each paper clearly labelled with a reference number
  - Table of contents with page references
  - Confidential watermark on sensitive documents
  - Proofread every page for errors

STEP 3: DISTRIBUTE THE BOARD PACK
  - Send via secure method (encrypted email, board portal)
  - Include a cover letter from the Chairperson
  - Confirm receipt from all directors
  - Send reminder 48 hours before the meeting with any late additions

STEP 4: MEETING DAY LOGISTICS
  - Arrive 30 minutes early to set up the room
  - Set up: Name placards, printed packs (if physical), AV, refreshments
  - Test video conferencing for remote attendees
  - Prepare a sign-in sheet for attendance records
  - Have spare copies of all documents available

STEP 5: TAKE MINUTES DURING THE MEETING
  Record:
  - Date, time, location, attendees, apologies
  - Each agenda item discussed: Key points, different views expressed
  - Decisions made: Exact wording of resolutions (passed/not passed)
  - Voting results: For, against, abstentions (if formal vote)
  - Action items: What, who, by when
  - Next meeting date and time
  - Time of adjournment

  Minute-taking tips:
  - Focus on DECISIONS and ACTIONS, not every word spoken
  - Use neutral, objective language
  - Capture the resolution verbatim when proposed
  - Note any conflicts of interest declared
  - If unsure, ask for clarification during the meeting

STEP 6: POST-MEETING (Within 24-48 hours)
  - Draft the minutes and submit to the Chairperson for review
  - Incorporate feedback and finalise
  - Distribute draft minutes to all board members within 5 business days
  - Extract action items and send to responsible parties with deadlines
  - Update the action items register
  - File all meeting documents securely
  - Begin preparation for the next meeting cycle

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Board portal software (Diligent, BoardEffect, Convene)
- Document formatting templates (Word with house style)
- Minutes template with standard headings
- Action item tracker (Excel or project management tool)
- Secure document sharing (encrypted email, board portal)
- AV and video conferencing equipment
- Recording device (if permitted by the board)

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Board pack delivery: 100% on time (5 business days before)
- Formatting: Zero errors, consistent throughout
- Minutes distribution: Within 5 business days of meeting
- Action item tracking: 100% captured and assigned
- Confidentiality: Zero breaches of board information
- Attendance record: Complete and accurate
