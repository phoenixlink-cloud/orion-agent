SKILL: APPOINTMENT SCHEDULING & CALENDAR COORDINATION

SKILL ID:       REC-SK-003
ROLE:           Receptionist / Front Desk Coordinator
CATEGORY:       Organisation & Planning
DIFFICULTY:     Intermediate
ESTIMATED TIME: Ongoing (daily task)

DESCRIPTION
Proficiently managing appointment scheduling across multiple calendars for
managers, meeting rooms, and shared resources. This includes booking, 
rescheduling, cancelling, sending reminders, resolving conflicts, and ensuring
meeting rooms are prepared with the correct equipment and refreshments.

OBJECTIVES
1. Schedule appointments accurately with zero double-bookings
2. Send calendar invites with complete details (agenda, location, dial-in)
3. Resolve scheduling conflicts within 15 minutes of detection
4. Send reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before meetings
5. Prepare meeting rooms (AV, refreshments, materials) 15 minutes before start
6. Maintain a daily meeting schedule visible to all relevant staff

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

STEP 1: RECEIVE THE BOOKING REQUEST
  - Capture: Who is requesting, attendees, purpose, preferred date/time,
    duration, room requirements (capacity, AV, whiteboard, video conf)
  - Confirm: Internal or external attendees? Catering needed?

STEP 2: CHECK AVAILABILITY
  - Open the shared calendar system (Outlook/Google Calendar)
  - Check availability of: All attendees, the preferred meeting room,
    any required equipment (projector, video conferencing unit)
  - If conflicts exist, propose 2-3 alternative time slots

STEP 3: BOOK THE MEETING
  - Create the calendar event with:
    * Clear subject line: "[Type] - [Topic] - [Host Name]"
    * Location: Room name + floor/building
    * Duration: Include 5-minute buffer for room turnover
    * Attendees: All participants (required and optional)
    * Body: Agenda, dial-in/video link, parking instructions for externals
    * Attachments: Any pre-read documents
  - Send the invite and confirm receipt with the requestor

STEP 4: SEND REMINDERS
  - 24 hours before: Email reminder to all attendees with any updated details
  - 1 hour before: Brief reminder via Teams/Slack to the meeting host
  - For external visitors: Include building directions, parking info, and
    check-in instructions in the 24-hour reminder

STEP 5: PREPARE THE ROOM
  - 15 minutes before: Verify room is clean and chairs are arranged
  - Set up AV equipment: Test projector, screen sharing, video conferencing
  - Place refreshments: Water, cups, notepads, pens
  - For catering meetings: Confirm delivery time with caterer, set up food
  - Place printed agendas at each seat (if requested by host)

STEP 6: HANDLE CHANGES
  - Rescheduling: Update the calendar event, notify all attendees, rebook room
  - Cancellation: Cancel the event, release the room, cancel catering orders
  - Running late: Notify waiting attendees with an estimated new start time
  - Room change: Update the invite, place signage at the original room door

STEP 7: POST-MEETING
  - Verify the room is cleared and reset for the next booking
  - Report any equipment issues to IT/facilities
  - Log catering costs for budget tracking

SCHEDULING CONFLICT RESOLUTION MATRIX

CONFLICT TYPE          RESOLUTION
--------------------   -------------------------------------------------------
Double-booked room     Move lower-priority meeting; offer alternative room
Exec calendar clash    Consult with executive assistant before rescheduling
External guest clash   Prioritise external guest; reschedule internal meeting
Equipment unavailable  Source backup equipment or move to equipped room
Catering conflict      Adjust catering order timing; use backup caterer

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Microsoft Outlook / Google Calendar
- Room booking system (Robin, Condeco, or integrated calendar)
- Video conferencing platforms (Teams, Zoom, Google Meet)
- Catering vendor contact list
- Meeting room equipment inventory list
- Printed daily meeting schedule (backup)

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Double-booking rate: 0%
- Room readiness: 100% prepared 15 minutes before
- Reminder delivery: 100% at 24h and 1h marks
- Conflict resolution time: < 15 minutes
- Attendee satisfaction: >= 4.3/5.0

BEST PRACTICES
- Block 10-minute buffers between back-to-back meetings for room turnover
- Maintain a preferred caterer list with menus and lead times
- Keep a spare set of AV cables and adapters at reception
- Print the next day's meeting schedule before leaving each evening
- Colour-code calendar entries: Red = external, Blue = internal, Green = recurring
