SKILL: BOARD MANAGEMENT & CORPORATE GOVERNANCE

SKILL ID:       CEO-SK-003
ROLE:           Chief Executive Officer
CATEGORY:       Governance & Stakeholder Management
DIFFICULTY:     Expert
ESTIMATED TIME: 4-8 hours per month (ongoing)

DESCRIPTION
Managing the relationship with the Board of Directors, ensuring effective
corporate governance, preparing and presenting board packs, implementing
board decisions, and maintaining the trust and confidence of directors and
shareholders. This skill ensures the organisation operates with transparency,
accountability, and sound governance practices.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

STEP 1: UNDERSTAND THE BOARD'S ROLE AND YOUR RELATIONSHIP
  The Board's responsibilities:
  - Approve strategy and major decisions
  - Oversee CEO and executive performance
  - Ensure financial integrity and risk management
  - Protect shareholder interests
  - Ensure legal and regulatory compliance
  - Appoint/remove the CEO (their most important decision)

  The CEO's relationship with the Board:
  - You REPORT TO the Board â€” they are your boss
  - No surprises: The Board should never learn bad news from the press
  - Transparency: Share the full picture, not a curated version
  - Seek guidance: Use the Board's experience and networks
  - Disagree respectfully: Present your recommendation, accept the decision
  - Execute: Implement Board decisions faithfully, even if you disagreed

STEP 2: PREPARE THE BOARD PACK
  Quarterly board pack (distributed 5 days before the meeting):

  1. CEO REPORT (2-3 pages):
     - Executive summary: Key highlights and lowlights
     - Strategic progress: Status of strategic initiatives
     - Key decisions made since last meeting
     - Risks and challenges: What keeps you up at night
     - Outlook: What's ahead in the next quarter

  2. FINANCIAL REPORT (CFO):
     - P&L: Actual vs. budget vs. prior year
     - Balance sheet and cash flow
     - Key financial metrics and ratios
     - Financial forecast update
     - Capital expenditure report

  3. OPERATIONAL HIGHLIGHTS (by function):
     - Sales pipeline and revenue outlook
     - Product/service updates
     - Customer metrics (NPS, churn, satisfaction)
     - HR: Headcount, attrition, engagement, key hires
     - Technology: Major initiatives, incidents, security

  4. RISK REGISTER:
     - Top 10 enterprise risks with movement indicators
     - New risks identified since last meeting
     - Mitigation status updates

  5. COMPLIANCE & GOVERNANCE:
     - Regulatory update
     - Compliance status
     - Legal matters (litigation, contracts)

  6. DECISION ITEMS:
     - Clear proposals requiring Board approval
     - Each with: Background, options, recommendation, financial impact, risk

STEP 3: FACILITATE EFFECTIVE BOARD MEETINGS
  Before the meeting:
  - Pre-brief the Chair on any sensitive topics
  - Ensure all directors have received and read the pack
  - Brief your C-suite presenters on their sections and likely questions
  - Anticipate tough questions and prepare answers

  During the meeting:
  - Keep to the agenda and time allocations
  - Present with confidence but welcome challenge
  - Direct questions to the appropriate C-suite member
  - Take clear notes on decisions, actions, and concerns
  - For decision items: Present the recommendation clearly, facilitate discussion,
    call for the decision

  After the meeting:
  - Distribute minutes within 5 business days
  - Track all action items to completion
  - Follow up on director requests or concerns
  - Begin preparation for the next meeting

STEP 4: MANAGE BOARD COMPOSITION AND EFFECTIVENESS
  Board effectiveness:
  - Annual Board evaluation: Self-assessment or externally facilitated
  - Assess: Is the Board composition right? (Skills, diversity, independence)
  - Ensure appropriate committees: Audit, Remuneration, Nomination, Risk
  - Recommend new director appointments to fill skills gaps
  - Director onboarding: Comprehensive induction for new Board members
  - Ongoing education: Keep directors informed on industry and regulatory developments

STEP 5: INVESTOR RELATIONS (If applicable)
  - Prepare and deliver quarterly earnings calls / investor updates
  - Maintain relationships with key shareholders and analysts
  - Annual General Meeting: Presentation, Q&A, resolutions
  - Respond to shareholder concerns and activist investors
  - Ensure consistent messaging between Board, investors, and public
  - Comply with disclosure requirements (if listed)

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Board management platform: Diligent, BoardEffect, Nasdaq Boardvantage
- Board pack template: Standardised format approved by the Chair
- Minutes template: Action-focused with clear decision records
- Board evaluation tools: External facilitators, survey platforms
- Governance frameworks: King IV, UK Corporate Governance Code, SOX
- Investor relations platform: For listed companies

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Board pack: Distributed 5+ days before every meeting
- Minutes: Circulated within 5 business days of meeting
- Action items: 100% tracked and reported at next meeting
- Board evaluation: Completed annually
- No surprises: Zero instances of the Board learning material news externally
- Governance compliance: 100% with applicable governance codes
- Director satisfaction: Positive assessment of CEO's Board management
