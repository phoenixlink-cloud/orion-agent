SKILL: INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL PLANNING & LOGISTICS

SKILL ID:       EA-SK-002
ROLE:           Executive Assistant
CATEGORY:       Travel & Logistics
DIFFICULTY:     Advanced
ESTIMATED TIME: 4-8 hours per trip

DESCRIPTION
Planning, booking, and managing complex domestic and international travel for
C-suite executives including flights, accommodation, ground transport, visa
requirements, travel insurance, and contingency planning. This skill covers
the full lifecycle from initial request through post-trip expense reconciliation.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

STEP 1: RECEIVE TRAVEL REQUEST
  Capture: Destination(s), dates, purpose, attendees, budget class, preferences
  (airline loyalty, hotel chain, seat preference, dietary requirements)

STEP 2: RESEARCH & BOOK
  - Flights: Compare options; book preferred carrier/alliance for points
    * Always book flexible/refundable fares for executives
    * Confirm visa requirements for all destinations
    * Check passport validity (6+ months from travel date)
  - Hotels: Book preferred chain; confirm late check-in/early checkout
    * Request executive floor, quiet room, specific bed type
  - Ground transport: Airport transfers, car service, rental car
    * Pre-book meet-and-greet for international arrivals
  - Visas: Submit applications 4-6 weeks in advance; track status
  - Travel insurance: Confirm corporate policy covers destination/activities

STEP 3: BUILD THE TRAVEL PACK
  Compile a single document containing:
  - Complete itinerary: Flights, hotels, meetings, dinners (chronological)
  - Booking confirmations and reference numbers
  - Contact details: Hotels, drivers, local office contacts, embassy
  - Meeting briefs: Purpose, attendees, preparation notes
  - Destination info: Weather, currency, tipping customs, emergency numbers
  - Maps and directions for key locations
  - Copies of passport, visa, insurance (digital and printed)

STEP 4: MANAGE DURING TRAVEL
  - Monitor flight status; alert executive to delays/changes
  - Be available for rebooking if flights are cancelled
  - Coordinate schedule changes with local contacts
  - Handle any emergencies: Medical, lost luggage, document issues

STEP 5: POST-TRIP
  - Collect receipts and compile expense report within 5 business days
  - Submit for reimbursement per company policy
  - File travel documents for records
  - Capture lessons learned (preferred hotels, transport tips)
  - Update the executive's loyalty programme accounts

TOOLS & RESOURCES
- Corporate travel management company (CWT, BCD, Amex GBT)
- Airline and hotel booking platforms
- TripIt or Concur for itinerary management
- Visa processing service
- Travel pack template (Word/PDF)
- World time zone converter
- Currency converter
- Emergency contact list template

QUALITY STANDARDS
- Booking accuracy: 100% (correct dates, names, preferences)
- Travel pack delivery: 48 hours before departure
- Visa processing: Zero missed deadlines
- Expense report: Submitted within 5 business days of return
- Executive satisfaction: Zero complaints about arrangements
